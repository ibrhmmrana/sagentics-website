# Case Study: Unified Donor Engagement System for a South African Nonprofit

## Overview

A leading nonprofit in South Africa relied on manual donor tracking across multiple channels. As donation volume grew, the team struggled to keep conversations, follow ups, and payment outcomes connected. Important moments were being missed, response times were inconsistent, and leadership had limited visibility into what was working.

Sagentics designed and shipped an integrated donor engagement system that connects WhatsApp, agentic calls, email, and payment flows into one CRM. Every interaction is logged, visible, and actionable from first donation through long term support.

## Client Profile

* Sector: Nonprofit, donor funded organisation
* Location: South Africa
* Primary channels: WhatsApp, email, phone calls, online payments
* Core need: A single system of record for donor relationships, with automation that improves response speed and recovers lost revenue

## The Challenge

### What was happening

* Donor conversations lived in silos, WhatsApp threads, inboxes, spreadsheets, and call notes
* Payment outcomes were not consistently tied back to the person and the conversation
* Abandoned donations were difficult to detect, and even harder to recover
* Staff had no unified timeline of donor history, which slowed support and reduced personalisation
* Reporting was fragmented, so leadership could not see engagement trends or drop off points

## Goals and Success Metrics

Sagentics aligned the build around measurable outcomes:

* Track every donor interaction across channels in one CRM record
* Monitor total donations and identify abandoned donations automatically
* Reduce time to first response after donation
* Increase donor engagement over time through scheduled impact updates
* Recover a meaningful share of abandoned donations within weeks

## Build Approach

Sagentics used tight build loops focused on speed and leverage:

1. Define the metric and the bottleneck
2. Ship a small vertical slice quickly
3. Test in the real world with live traffic
4. Measure what changed
5. Iterate relentlessly

This ensured the system improved outcomes continuously, not just features.

All interactions are stored in the CRM with deal tracking, statuses, and next actions so the team always knows what to do next.

**5) Multi channel execution**
WhatsApp, email, and agentic calls are orchestrated as one journey, with consistent context and outcomes flowing back to the CRM.

## Key Automations Delivered

* **Personalised thank you messages within 30 minutes of a donation**
  Triggered on successful payment. Uses donor context and donation details. Logged automatically in the CRM.

* **Automated impact updates**
  Scheduled messages at 40 days, 6 months, and 1 year. Each update is recorded and linked to the donor timeline, so the team can see what was sent and when.

* **Abandoned donation detection and recovery sequences**
  Detects incomplete payment flows, creates a recovery opportunity in the CRM, then runs a sequence across WhatsApp and email. Escalates to agentic calls for high value cases.

* **Deal tracking inside the CRM**
  Donors and donation opportunities are tracked with clear statuses, ownership, and next steps. Staff can pick up any conversation with full context.

* **Multi channel communication with unified context**
  Messages and calls reference the same donor history, which reduces repetition and improves donor experience.

## Results

### The numbers

* **6,876 interactions** tracked and stored in the CRM
* **R22.3 million** in donations monitored
* **R582k** in abandoned donations identified
* **33%** of abandoned donations recovered within 3 weeks
* **17%** increase in donor engagement

### What changed operationally

* The team moved from manual chasing to a system that surfaces priorities automatically
* Donors received faster, more consistent communication
* Leadership gained visibility into engagement, recovery performance, and pipeline health
* Recovery became a repeatable process, not an occasional effort
